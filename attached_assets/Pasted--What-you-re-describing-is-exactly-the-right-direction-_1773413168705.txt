
What you’re describing is exactly the right direction. If you make Evolv feel like Intuit’s TurboTax experience, people will complete the process themselves while your system does the heavy lifting. That’s the key to scale.

The idea is to turn credit repair into a guided step-by-step journey, where users answer questions and your system automatically generates disputes, complaints, and actions.

Below is a complete blueprint for a TurboTax-style DIY credit repair experience inside Evolv.

⸻

1️⃣ The Core Concept

Instead of a dashboard first, users see a guided wizard.

Think of it like:

Welcome to Evolv

Step 1: Import your credit report
Step 2: Detect violations
Step 3: Choose dispute strategy
Step 4: Generate disputes
Step 5: Track progress
Step 6: Improve score

Users always see a progress bar.

Example:

Credit Repair Progress
██████░░░░ 60%

This keeps them engaged.

⸻

2️⃣ Step 1 — Import Credit Report

First screen:

“Let’s analyze your credit report.”

Options:
	•	Upload PDF
	•	Connect credit monitoring
	•	Enter accounts manually

After upload your AI parser extracts:

• accounts
• balances
• dates
• creditor names
• bureau data

⸻

3️⃣ Step 2 — Violation Detection

Now the system shows detected problems.

Example screen:

We found 12 possible reporting violations

Cards show:

⚠ Date opened mismatch
⚠ Balance inconsistent across bureaus
⚠ Possible re-aging
⚠ Duplicate account

Each item has:

[Explain violation]   [Dispute]

When clicked:

A simple explanation appears.

Example:

“This account shows different open dates across bureaus. Under FCRA §623, furnishers must report accurate data.”

This makes the system feel smart and authoritative.

⸻

4️⃣ Step 3 — Strategy Selection

Now the user chooses how aggressive they want to be.

Example screen:

Choose your dispute strategy

Basic Strategy
Standard bureau dispute.

Advanced Strategy
Metro-2 violation dispute.

Legal Strategy
Dispute + CFPB complaint.

This is where you upsell.

Example:

Basic Plan
$0

Advanced Plan
$49

Pro Plan
$99


⸻

5️⃣ Step 4 — Automatic Dispute Generation

The system now generates letters.

Example:

Dispute Letters Ready

List:

Equifax dispute
Experian dispute
TransUnion dispute

Preview option.

Buttons:

Send digitally
Download
Mail with certified service

Your CRM already supports dispute automation, so this fits perfectly.

⸻

6️⃣ Step 5 — Progress Tracker

Now the interface becomes a tracker.

Example timeline:

Initial Dispute Sent ✓
Waiting for Bureau Response
Follow-up Round
Results Review
Final Resolution

This creates gamification.

⸻

7️⃣ Step 6 — Score Improvement Tools

Now you guide them to improvement tools.

Example screen:

Improve Your Score Faster

Suggestions:

• Add tradeline
• Reduce utilization
• Open credit builder account
• Remove inquiries

Each action shows estimated score impact.

Example:

Add tradeline
Estimated score increase: +35


⸻

8️⃣ Credit Score Simulator (Extremely Powerful)

This is the killer feature.

User enters:
	•	remove collections
	•	add tradeline
	•	pay down cards

System shows projection.

Example:

Current Score: 505
Projected Score: 672

This motivates users.

⸻

9️⃣ Gamification (TurboTax-style)

Give them milestones.

Example badges:

✓ Credit report uploaded
✓ Violations detected
✓ First dispute sent
✓ Score improved

This keeps engagement high.

⸻

Why This Model Works

The TurboTax experience works because:

• users feel in control
• the system guides decisions
• complexity is hidden
• progress is visible

If Evolv adopts this flow, it becomes:

“TurboTax for Credit Repair.”

That positioning alone is extremely powerful.
