Here’s a straight list of legitimate primary tradelines and credit-building accounts you can open in your own name that report to the major credit bureaus and help build your credit history (backdating only applies to authorized user accounts, not to primary tradelines you open yourself):

⸻

🧱 Credit-Builder Loan Accounts

These are loans where your on-time payments create a positive tradeline on your credit report.  ￼
	1.	Self Credit Builder Account – Reports to all three bureaus and helps build payment history.  ￼
	2.	CreditStrong Credit Builder Loan – Longer terms and higher amounts.  ￼
	3.	BMO Credit Builder Loan – A traditional bank credit builder loan that reports payments.  ￼
	4.	Digital Federal Credit Union (DCU) Credit Builder Loan – Fixed-rate, traditional credit builder option.  ￼
	5.	Credit Karma Credit Builder – No fees credit builder program.  ￼
	6.	Local Credit Union Credit Builder Loans (e.g., Alltru, USAlliance, UCSCU, Civic Federal CU) – Often flexible, low minimums.  ￼

📌 Tip: Making on-time monthly payments on these is what builds your payment-history tradeline.  ￼

⸻

💳 Secured Credit Cards (Primary Tradelines)

Secured cards require a security deposit and are easier to get with thin or bad credit; activity reports as a primary tradeline.  ￼
	1.	Chime® Credit Builder Secured Visa® Credit Card – Designed specifically for building credit.  ￼
	2.	Capital One Quicksilver Secured Cash Rewards – Reports regularly and earns rewards.  ￼
	3.	Capital One Platinum Secured Credit Card – No annual fee and good for credit building.  ￼
	4.	Discover it® Secured Credit Card – Reports to all bureaus and offers cash back.  ￼
	5.	Varo Visa® Believe Credit Card – Alternative secured card focusing on credit building.  ￼
	6.	Navy Federal cashRewards Secured Card – Secured card with cash back and reporting.  ￼
	7.	OpenSky® Secured Visa Credit Card – Easier approval (no credit check in some cases).  ￼
	8.	Other options for poor/no credit: Grow Credit Mastercard, Mission Lane Green Line Visa, Tilt Motion, etc.  ￼

⸻

📱 Credit-Building Apps / Alternative Reporting

These are tools that report payments to build tradelines, some even on non-traditional data like subscriptions or rent.  ￼
	1.	Grow Credit – Reports subscription payments as tradelines.  ￼
	2.	Experian Boost – Adds on-time utility, cell, and other bills to your report.  ￼
	3.	Ava (credit-building service/app) – Reports to all three bureaus quickly after joining.  ￼

⸻

🏠 Rent-Reporting Services

These don’t open a typical loan/card tradeline, but rent reporting can add a positive tradeline (including past on-time rent history with some services).  ￼
	•	Esusu
	•	TurboTenant
	•	PayYourRent
	•	Bilt Rewards (rent reporting)

(Some will even report up to 24 months of historical on-time rent payments.)  ￼

⸻

⚠️ What about “backdated primary tradelines”?

Legitimate primary tradelines are accounts you open yourself — they can only start reporting when the account is active. Any offer claiming to add years of backdated history on a primary tradeline is typically not legitimate. The only way to get older history is through authorized user tradelines from someone else’s account — but those are not primary tradelines.  ￼

⸻

📌 Most Effective Strategy

To build real credit history that stands up to lenders:
	1.	Open one or more credit-builder loans and pay on time.
	2.	Open a secured credit card and maintain low utilization.
	3.	Use apps and rent reporting to add diversified tradelines.
	4.	Make every payment on time — payment history is the biggest factor in your credit score.
